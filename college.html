College Offered Tracks

Track Description
Bachelor of Science in Information Technology/ Associate in Computer Technology The Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) and Associate in Computer Technology (ACT) programs offer comprehensive education in computer systems, software development, networking, and information security. These programs equip students with the technical skills and theoretical knowledge necessary to succeed in various IT-related fields. From programming languages to database management, students learn to analyze, design, and implement IT solutions effectively.
Bachelor of Arts in English Language Studies The Bachelor of Arts in English Language Studies program explores the English language's structure, usage, and cultural significance. Through courses in linguistics, literature, and language acquisition, students analyze grammar, literature, and language teaching methods. This program prepares graduates for careers in education, communication, and publishing, emphasizing critical thinking and communication skills.
Bachelor of Technical Vocational Teacher Education A Bachelor of Technical Vocational Teacher Education program prepares graduates to teach technical subjects like automotive technology, culinary arts, and construction trades. It combines theoretical knowledge with practical training, focusing on pedagogical techniques and industry-specific skills. Graduates are equipped to inspire and guide students toward successful careers in vocational fields.
Computer Hardware Servicing Computer Hardware Servicing involves maintaining, repairing, and optimizing computer hardware components for optimal performance and functionality.
Weilding and Fabrication Technology Welding and Fabrication Technology involves joining metal parts and shaping them into structures using welding and fabrication techniques. It's vital for creating durable metal products in industries like construction and manufacturing.
